SASSA R350 Grant Status: How to Check and What It Means? The SASSA R350 Social Relief of Distress (SRD) grant was introduced to provide temporary financial assistance to unemployed South Africans during difficult times, especially in response to the COVID-19 pandemic. If you’ve applied for the R350 grant, it’s important to regularly check your application status to ensure you receive your benefits without delay. This guide will walk you through how to check your SASSA R350 grant status, what the different statuses mean, and what to do if you encounter any issues.

How to Check Your SASSA R350 Grant Status?

You can check the status of your R350 SRD grant application through several convenient methods:

1. Online Status Check

One of the easiest ways to check your status is online:

1. Visit the official SRD SASSA website: srd.sassa.gov.za.

2. Scroll down to the section labeled “Application Status.”

3. Enter your South African ID number and the phone number you used to register your application.

4. Click “Submit” to view the status of your application.

2. Check via WhatsApp

You can also check your SASSA R350 status using WhatsApp:

1. Save the SASSA WhatsApp number: 082 046 8553.

2. Send a message with the word “Status” to this number.

3. Follow the prompts by entering your ID number.

4. You will receive a message with your current application status.

3. Check via SMS

To check your R350 status via SMS:

1. Send an SMS with your ID number to 082 046 8553.

2. SASSA will reply with your application status.

4. Call the SASSA Helpline

If you prefer to speak with someone directly:

• Toll-Free Number: 0800 60 10 11

• Follow the voice prompts to check your R350 grant status.

Understanding Your SASSA R350 Grant Status

When you check your status, you might see one of the following updates:

• Pending: Your application is still being processed. SASSA is verifying your details and will update your status once the review is complete.

• Approved: Your application has been approved. You should see the payment date and other relevant details.

• Declined: Unfortunately, your application was declined. The reason for the decline will be provided. Common reasons include failing to meet the eligibility criteria or issues with your documentation.

• Payment in Progress: Your payment is being processed and will be disbursed shortly.

• Paid: The payment has been successfully processed and should reflect in your account or be available for collection.

What to Do If Your SASSA R350 Application Is Declined?

If your application is declined, don’t panic—you can appeal the decision. Here’s what you need to do:

1. Review the Reason for Decline

When your application is declined, SASSA provides a reason. Common reasons include:

• Receiving other income or benefits that disqualify you.

• Incorrect or incomplete application details.

2. Submit an Appeal

You can submit an appeal through the SRD website. Here’s how:

1. Visit srd.sassa.gov.za.

2. Scroll to the “Application for Reconsideration” section.

3. Enter your ID number and mobile number.

4. Follow the prompts to submit your appeal, providing any additional information or documentation required.

3. Wait for the Appeal Outcome

After submitting your appeal, SASSA will review the decision and notify you of the outcome. This process can take several weeks, so patience is key.

What to Do If Your Payment Is Delayed?

If your status shows “Payment in Progress” for an extended period, or if you haven’t received your payment, try the following:

• Verify Your Payment Details: Ensure that the bank account details or payment method you provided are correct.

• Check Your Balance: If you receive payments through a SASSA card, check your balance at an ATM or participating retailer.

• Contact SASSA: Reach out to SASSA through the helpline or visit your nearest office if your payment is significantly delayed.

Tips for Managing Your SASSA R350 Grant

• Regularly Check Your Status: Stay informed about your application status by checking regularly.

• Keep Your Information Updated: Ensure that SASSA has your correct phone number and bank details to avoid payment issues.

• Use Your SASSA Card Wisely: If you receive payments through a SASSA card, use it at participating retailers to avoid fees.
